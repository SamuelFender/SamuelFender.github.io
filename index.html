Operational Framework

THE RAILGUARD LOOP

Every second of operation, RailGuard AI cycles through four integrated phases — transforming raw sensor data into decisive action.

01

DETECT

Autonomous drones patrol assigned rail corridors on scheduled intervals, capturing high-resolution visual, thermal, and LiDAR data. Simultaneously, onboard sensors monitor bearing temperature, vibration, brake pressure, and structural stress across every locomotive and freight car — mile by mile.

02

DECIDE

The AI synthesizes all data streams into the Predictive Failure Risk Index (PFRI). Every asset receives a real-time risk score: Monitor, Route to next yard, or Stop immediately. The system decides whether to trigger maintenance scheduling, fuel cell switching, or emergency response protocols.

03

ACT

Automatically dispatches prioritized alerts to maintenance crews, triggers parts procurement, generates work orders in existing CMMS systems, and — when PFRI reaches critical threshold — activates stabilization mechanisms to slow or halt the train and dispatches emergency engineers en route.

04

EXPLAIN

Every decision and alert is logged with full traceability: sensor readings, PFRI scores, recommended and executed actions, and outcomes. The unified dashboard provides regulators, safety managers, and rail operators with a transparent, auditable record of fleet and track health across the entire network.

Emergency Response Protocol

WHEN IT MATTERS MOST

If anything goes wrong, RailGuard AI doesn't just alert — it acts. Stabilization mechanisms, emergency dispatch, and full accountability in one coordinated response.

01

PFRI THRESHOLD BREACH

Sensors detect anomaly — bearing overtemperature, rail stress fracture, or missing fastener. PFRI score crosses critical threshold. Alert generated within 30 seconds.

02

STABILIZATION ACTIVATED

Stabilization mechanisms engage in conjunction with onboard sensors — slowing or halting the train to prevent derailment while engineers are mobilized. Train does not proceed until clearance confirmed.

03

EMERGENCY ENGINEERS DISPATCHED

Emergency maintenance engineers are automatically dispatched en route. Precise asset location, defect type, and recommended action transmitted to crew in real time.

04

DRONE SECONDARY ASSESSMENT

Nearest available drone is rerouted to the incident area for aerial assessment — providing engineers visual confirmation of the threat before they arrive on site.

05

FULL INCIDENT LOG & CLEARANCE

All actions, sensor readings, and decisions are logged in the unified dashboard. Operations management and regulators receive a complete, auditable incident report. Train cleared for operation upon confirmation.
